FAQ

What does "Professor" mean?
It is a Portuguese word meaning teacher. The title is bestowed upon an "instrutor" (or a student that provides instruction to other students) that has attained a level of proficiency in the teaching of Capoeira to beginners and advanced students. Zumbi was awarded the title of professor in 2015 from Mestre Chicote, a very famous Capoeira master from Brazil that now teaches in Europe. Professor Zumbi received his professor cord in the presence of Capoeira grand masters, Mestre Suassuna and Mestre Acordeon.
What does "Zumbi" mean?
Zumbi (1655 - November 20, 1695), also known as Zumbi dos Palmares, was the last of the leaders of the Quilombo dos Palmares, in the present-day state of Alagoas, Brazil. [source: Wikipedia]
What does "CDO" mean?
CDO stands for Cordão de Ouro which was the name given to a legendary fighter in Brazil. It is also the name of Zumbi's capoeira group, the capoeira group started by Mestre Suassuna, a legend himself.

Professor Zumbi hails from the island of Jamaica and leads the Cordão de Ouro Capoeira group in Seoul. Zumbi has practiced and taught Capoeira on every continent since commencing his capoeira voyage in 2000 in Berkeley, California. He is presently planning to teach private classes in Hong Kong and provide introductory courses in Mount Dora, Florida.

Prior to retiring from the corporate world to teach capoeira professionally, Zumbi was a business development manager at SK Telecom. Zumbi graduated from the Wharton School with an MBA in Marketing. He credits his discplined training and beloved Capoeira community as major contributors to his professional and academic success.
